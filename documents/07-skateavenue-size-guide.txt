What Size Skateboard Should I Get? (Quick Answer)

The right skateboard size depends on your shoe size, height, and skating style . Generally, beginners should start with an 8.0” – 8.25” deck for a balance of stability and control. If you prefer street skating and tricks , go for a 7.5” – 8.0” width , while cruising and transition skating require a wider deck (8.25” – 8.5”+). Choosing the correct skateboard size enhances your performance, balance, and comfort on the board. Keep reading for a detailed size chart and expert recommendations!

Factors to Consider When Choosing a Skateboard Size

There’s no one-size-fits-all when it comes to skateboards. However, three main factors should guide your decision:

Riding Style & Terrain – Do you skate street, parks, pools, or just cruise?

Shoe Size & Body Proportions – The right deck width depends on your foot size.

Deck Width & Length – This determines your board’s maneuverability and stability.

1. Skateboard Size by Riding Style

Your skating style plays a huge role in picking the right board size:

Street & Technical Tricks – If you love flip tricks, rails, and ledges, opt for a narrower deck (7.5” – 8.0”) for easier control.

Park & Transition Skating – Ramps, pools, and bowls require a wider deck (8.0” – 8.5”) for better stability.

Cruising & Transportation – If you mainly cruise around, a wider deck (8.25” and up) offers a smoother ride.

2. Skateboard Size by Shoe Size

Your shoe size is one of the most crucial factors in picking the right skateboard.

Why Does Shoe Size Matters?

Too narrow? Your feet will hang over the edges, making it hard to balance.

Too wide? The board becomes heavy and harder to maneuver.

4. Skateboard Length & Wheelbase

Standard deck length : 28” – 33” (Most street & transition boards)

Longer decks : Offer more stability (Best for ramps & cruising)

Shorter decks : Are easier to control (Great for younger skaters)

The wheelbase (distance between trucks) also affects turning ability, but for most skaters, deck width is the main factor to focus on.

Final Tips for Choosing the Right Skateboard Size

Try Different Sizes – If possible, test different deck widths to see what feels best.

Consider Your Comfort – Choose a size that provides balance and control.

Think About Progression – If you’re a beginner, go for a slightly wider deck to improve stability.

Conclusion: What’s the Best Skateboard Size for You?

Finding the right skateboard size depends on your shoe size, height, riding style, and personal preference . If you love street skating, go for a 7.5” – 8.0” board. If you prefer ramps or cruising, choose an 8.25”+ deck for stability.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ) About Skateboard Size

1. What size skateboard should I get for my height?

Your height can help determine your skateboard size, but shoe size and riding style are more important. Generally:

Under 4’4” → 7.0” – 7.5” deck width

4’5” – 5’2” → 7.5” – 7.75” deck width

5’3” and taller → 7.75” – 8.5”+ deck width

2. What skateboard size is best for beginners?

For beginners, an 8.0” – 8.25” deck width is ideal. It offers a balance of stability and control , making it easier to learn basic tricks and cruising.

3. How does shoe size affect skateboard deck width?

Your shoe size should match your skateboard width for better balance and performance:

Size 6 – 9 (Men's US) → 7.5” – 7.75” deck width

Size 9 – 12+ (Men's US) → 8.0” – 8.5”+ deck width

4. What size skateboard is best for tricks?

For flip tricks and technical street skating , choose a narrower deck (7.5” – 8.0”) . It’s lighter and easier to maneuver.

5. What skateboard size is best for cruising?

For cruising and transportation , go for a wider deck (8.25” – 8.5”+). This provides better stability and comfort.

6. Does skateboard length matter?

For most skaters, deck width is more important than length. However:

Shorter boards (~28” – 31”) → More control (good for tricks)

Longer boards (~32” – 33”) → More stability (better for ramps & cruising)

7. What is the difference between a street skateboard and a cruiser?

Street skateboards → Narrower decks (7.5” – 8.25”), harder wheels for tricks.

Cruiser skateboards → Wider decks (8.5”+), softer wheels for smoother rides.

8. Can I ride a skateboard that’s too big or too small for me?

Yes, but it may affect your balance and control. Too small = unstable, harder to balance. Too big = heavy, harder to maneuver.

9. What size skateboard should kids get?

For younger riders, use this guide:

Under 6 years old → 6.5” – 7.0” width

6 – 12 years old → 7.0” – 7.5” width

Teenagers & adults → 7.75” – 8.5” width (depends on shoe size & style)

10. What skateboard size do professionals use?

Most pro skaters ride 7.75” – 8.5” decks , depending on their style:

Street pros → 7.75” – 8.25” for tricks

Transition/park pros → 8.25” – 8.5”+ for ramps & pools
